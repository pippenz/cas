## P1 (recommend fix before merge — in core scope)

- **Commit guards don't protect `epic/*`.** `pre_tool.rs`, `teams.rs` (pre-commit hook), `handlers_session.rs` all check a denylist `[main, master, staging]`. Workers fork from the supervisor's checkout HEAD, which in EPIC mode is `epic/<title>` — and the bug report's 2nd incident was a worker leaking onto the epic branch. Layer-1 cwd-scope check still catches the common case, but Layers 2/3 don't fire for `epic/*`.
  - **Fix:** flip the worker guards from denylist → **allowlist**: a worker may commit ONLY on its own `factory/<name>`; deny every other branch. Closes main/staging/epic/anything in one stroke. Owner: guards (A2 files).

## Cheap correctness (recommend fold in)

- **B4 PreCompact flush skipped on empty context** — `handle_pre_compact` early-returns at `if context_parts.is_empty()` BEFORE calling `flush_worker_findings_to_task` (notifications.rs). A worker with in-flight findings but no high-importance memories/tasks won't flush — the exact data loss B4 prevents. Fix: move the flush above the early return. Owner: surface (B4).
- **Test-lock consolidation incomplete** — `close_ops.rs` kept its private `env_lock()` (with poison-panicking `.lock().unwrap()`) after guards reverted its change (to avoid the merge conflict). Consolidation's "one lock" goal unmet; close_ops env-tests race against others. Fix: delegate to `crate::hooks::test_env_lock()`. Owner: surface (owns close_ops.rs).

## Fast-follow (file as a task; non-blocking)

- **No timeout on `gh pr list` / git subprocesses in async `factory_worker_status`** (factory_ops.rs ~1614/1700). Hung/slow `gh` stalls the supervisor's worker_status poll (worsens a pre-existing sync-git-in-async pattern; gated to pushed branches; degrades to stalled-recoverable, not crash). Fix: `tokio::time::timeout` + `spawn_blocking`, degrade pr_url→"none" on timeout.
- **Detached-HEAD fail-open across the 3 guards** (acceptable; document) and a **narrow reuse-path false-fail** — `WorkerSpawnPrep::run` `bail!`s when a reused worktree is in detached-HEAD (`current_branch()=="HEAD"`); previously-working dir now hard-fails. Consider treating detached as recoverable (`git switch factory/<name>`).
